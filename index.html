Pre-trial conference

Before the date of your trial is scheduled, you will have to attend a pre-trial conference to deal with administrative matters in preparation for trial.

What is a pre-trial conference

A case management hearing, which is often called a pre-trial conference (PTC), in other jurisdictions, is a virtual or real gathering between litigants prior to the trial before a judge or judicial authority.

Read More

What to expect

At the outset of the pre-trial conference, the Advocates, judges, and Clients will discuss the following requisites:
1. Whether the parties are ready to proceed to trial
2. The evidence that each party intends to present
3. Any motions that need to be resolved before trial
4. The possibility of a settlement of a case

Objectives of pre-trial conference

1. Expedite the disposition of the case
2. Help the court establish managerial control over the case
3. Discourage wasteful pre-trial activities
4. Improve the quality of the trial with thorough preparation
5. Facilitate a settlement of the case

Need Help?

The information here is for general guidance as the courts do not provide legal advice. If you need further help, you may want to get independent legal advice.
